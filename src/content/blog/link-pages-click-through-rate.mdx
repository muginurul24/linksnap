# How Link Pages 5x'd Our Click-Through Rate

A short link normally gets one chance to convince someone to continue: the slug.
That is not much room for brand, offer, or urgency.

Link Pages add a lightweight step between the click and the destination. The
visitor sees the brand, offer title, CTA, countdown timer, and optional QR code
before continuing. That step can make the redirect feel intentional instead of
anonymous.

## Why the extra step can help

The best Link Pages do not delay the user. They answer the question that appears
right before a click: "Is this link worth opening?"

For marketplace sellers, the answer might be social proof and a limited-time
offer. For event teams, it might be the event name and location. For creators, it
might be the affiliate product and the reason to buy today.

## What belongs on a Link Page

- A recognizable brand or campaign name.
- A short offer headline.
- One CTA that matches the destination.
- A timer only when there is a real deadline.
- Social proof when the click count is meaningful.
- A QR code when the campaign crosses offline and online channels.

## Keep it focused

Link Pages work because they are small. They should not become full landing
pages with competing sections. The job is to make the next click feel obvious.

When a short link becomes a branded offer step, click-through rate stops being a
property of the destination alone. The link starts doing conversion work before
the final page even loads.
